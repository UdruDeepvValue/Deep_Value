Problems with current M&A valuation

ERRORS WORTH MILLIONS

No matter if you are a buyer or a seller, even a small error in valuation can have a significant impact on your financial outcome

SUBJECTIVITY AND BIAS

Human judgment can introduce biases and inconsistencies, leading to inaccurate valuations

LIMITED DATA COVERAGE

Traditional valuation methods rely on limited data sets, such as financial statements, which may overlook important factors such as market dynamics, growth potential, and sector trends.

LACK OF TRANSPARENCY

Traditional valuation processes are often opaque, making it difficult for stakeholders to understand the rationale behind valuations.

TIME CONSUMING AND COSTLY

Manual valuation methods are often time-consuming and resource-intensive, increasing the overall cost of the M&A process

LACK OF M&A STRUCTURE

Many organizations lack a structured approach to M&A, leading to uncertainty in valuing businesses.

LACK OF TRUST IN VALUATION

Valuation professionals may lack confidence in presenting their results to senior stakeholders
